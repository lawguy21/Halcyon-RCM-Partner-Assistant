History of Present Illness

Ms. Thompson is a 50-year-old female with a significant past medical history of COPD (GOLD Stage III), type 2 diabetes, hypertension, CKD stage 3, and major depressive disorder who presented to the emergency department on 12/28/2025 with a 4-day history of worsening shortness of breath, productive cough with yellowish-green sputum, and low-grade fever.

  • ABG on admission: pH 7.32, pCO2 52, pO2 58, HCO3 26 (compensated respiratory acidosis)

She was started on IV ceftriaxone and azithromycin for community-acquired pneumonia, systemic corticosteroids (methylprednisolone 125mg IV daily), scheduled nebulizer treatments, and continued on supplemental oxygen.

Pulmonology was consulted and recommended continuation of long-acting bronchodilator therapy and consideration for pulmonary rehabilitation.

She was screened for Medicaid eligibility and connected with hospital financial assistance programs. Psychiatry was also consulted given her history of depression; her current antidepressant regimen was continued.

By hospital day 6, the patient was ambulating independently, tolerating oral intake, and maintaining oxygen saturation >92% on room air. She was deemed stable for discharge.
